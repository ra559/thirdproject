About Me

My name is Robert Alberto. I am from the Dominican Republic and came to live in the USA in Dec 2013. In the Dominican Republic, I used to work in the Hospitality business and when I emigrated to the USA I worked in the retail industry. I work for a software company in New York City as a Technical Support Representative and I am also a small business owner who specializes in providing IT solutions to small businesses and individuals. As a career goal, I want to be a DevOps Engineer and eventually grow my business to a point where I do not need to be employed but be an employer instead.

Shortly after arriving in the USA, I decided to go to college and major in IT. I went to Passaic County Community College where I obtained a Networking Administration Associates degree. After completion, I transferred to NJIT where I completed a BS in Engineering Technology with a concentration in IT. Without losing time, I enrolled in the Master’s program for IT Administration & Security. I am interested in all the technologies that surround the DevOps philosophy therefore I am cloud-oriented and a big Linux enthusiast (let’s Automate the world!).

For relaxation and hobbies, I read, play videogames, do exercise, play with my Raspberry Pi, learn new technologies, and hang out with my family (mostly to go buy books or eat). For reading, I enjoy a menage of topics that includes, but are not limited to, horror, classic literature, sci-fi, AI, history, and more.

About this project

This projects provides a brief description of How the internet works. It explores topics such as DNS, IP address, Routing, HTTP, HTML and CSS. The sources for compiling this information are: Computer Networks and Internets by Douglas E. Commer COMPTia Network + Study Guide by Todd Lemmle, HTTP (HyperText Transfer Protocol) by https://www.ntu.edu.sg/ and The Worlds largest Encyclopedia Wikipedia. Much of the information here has been paraprased and rearranged from their original sources to cover the topics mentioned before and make it beginners friendly.
